Blackjack Rules

Objective

Beat the dealer by not busting (going over 21) and either outscoring the dealer or having the dealer bust.

Rules

  1. Game is played with one deck of 52 cards.
  2. Aces = 1 or 11 points (up to the player), 2 to 9 according to their values, and 10s and face cards = 10 points.
  3. Value of a hand is the sum of the point values of the cards. Blackjack is the highest hand (an ace and any 10 point card, it outranks all other 21 point hands).
  4. Based on the required table minimum amount, the player makes a bet (required amount or higher).
  5. After the player bets and hits deal, the dealer will give 2 cards to the player and 2 cards to himself.
    • One of the dealer’s cards is dealt face up.
    • The other card is hidden or facedown (called the “hole card”).
  6. The play begins with the player. There are two choices available to the player:
    • Stand: the player is not dealt additional cards.
    • Hit: the player draws another card (and more if they want to).
      • If this card causes the player’s total points to go over 21 (“busting”) then they lose.
  7. If the player busts (goes over 21), the hole card remains hidden.
    • In all other cases, the hole card is flipped over.
  8. If the player busts, the dealer wins, and they lose all the chips they bet.
    • If the player runs out of chips, they will be asked if they want to play again.
    • If they agree to a new game, the board will be cleared and chips will be resettled to $500.
  9. If the dealer busts, the player gets the chips they bet.
  10. If the player has blackjack, the player wins, and gets the chips they bet.
    • The hole card is then flipped, and the game ends.
  11. If the dealer has blackjack, the dealer wins, and the player loses the chips they bet.
    • The hole card is then flipped, and the game ends.
  12. If the dealer does not have blackjack, the dealer takes another card.
    • If the dealer has less than 16, they take another card.
    • If the dealer has more than 16, then it is checked to see if the dealer has more than 21.
    • If the dealer has more than 21, the dealer busts, and the player wins. Then the hole card is flipped, and the game ends.
    • If the dealer has less than 21, then it is checked to see if the dealer has more points than the player.
    • If the dealer has more points than the player, the dealer wins, the hole card is flipped, and the game ends.
    • If not, then it is checked to see if the dealer and player have tied/ have equal points.
    • If they have equal points, it results in a push where the player gets their original bet back, the hole card is flipped, and the game ends.
    • If they do not have equal points, the dealer wins, the hole card is flipped, and the game ends.
  13. If the player AND the dealer both have blackjack, it results in a push where the player gets their original bet back.
    • The hole card is then flipped, and the game ends.
